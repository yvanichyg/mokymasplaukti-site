Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how personal data is processed when you visit mokymasplaukti.lt or contact us by phone, email, or via social media.

Data Controller

  • Service provider: Ivan Gvozdov
  • Business form: individual activity
  • Location: Vilnius, Lithuania
  • Contacts: info@mokymasplaukti.lt, +370 611 62333

What data we may collect

  • Contact data: name (if provided), phone number, email address, and message content.
  • Technical data: IP address, device/browser information, visit time and page views (in server logs).
  • Social media: if you message via Instagram/Facebook, we receive your profile name and message under those platforms’ rules.

Purposes of processing

  • to respond to enquiries and communicate with clients;
  • to arrange lesson time / location / format;
  • to ensure website security and stable operation;
  • to resolve disputes or legal matters (if needed).

Legal bases (GDPR)

  • Consent (when you voluntarily contact us).
  • Contract performance (when we agree on providing services).
  • Legitimate interests (website security, abuse prevention, basic server logs).
  • Legal obligation (e.g., accounting or legal requirements, where applicable).

How long we keep data

  • enquiries and messages — as long as needed to arrange/provide the service and reasonably afterwards (e.g., dispute prevention);
  • accounting documents — as required by law (where applicable);
  • technical server logs — for a limited time for security purposes.

Recipients of data

  • data may be processed by service providers (e.g., hosting, email) only to the extent necessary to deliver the service;
  • we do not sell or transfer personal data to third parties, except where required by law.

Cookies

Currently, the website uses only essential technical cookies required for proper operation. If analytics or advertising tools are added in the future, this policy will be updated.

Your rights

  • the right to access your personal data;
  • the right to request correction of inaccurate data;
  • the right to request deletion (where applicable);
  • the right to restrict processing (where applicable);
  • the right to data portability (where applicable);
  • the right to object to processing based on legitimate interests (where applicable);
  • the right to withdraw consent at any time (this does not affect processing before withdrawal).

Complaints

If you believe your rights have been violated, you may file a complaint with the State Data Protection Inspectorate of Lithuania (VDAI).
